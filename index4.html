What is Cyber Security ?

Cyber security is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These cyber attacks often aim to access, change, or destroy sensitive information, extort money from users, or interrupt normal business processes.
Here's a brief overview of key components and concepts in cyber security:

1. Types of Cyber Threats:

(i)Malware: Malicious software like viruses, worms, spyware, and ransomware that can damage systems or steal data.

(iii)Man-in-the-Middle Attack (MitM): An attacker intercepts and possibly alters the communication between two parties.

(iv)Denial of Service (DoS) Attack: Flooding a network or server with traffic to make it unavailable to users.

(v)SQL Injection: Inserting malicious SQL code into a server query to ac cess information.,?



2. Cyber Security Measures:

(i)Firewalls: Software or hardware that blocks unauthorized access while allowing outward communication.

(ii)Encryption: Converting data into a code to prevent unauthorized access.

(iii)Antivirus Software: Programs designed to detect and remove malware.

(iv)Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Requires multiple forms of verification to gain access to systems.

(v)Regular Updates: Keeping software and systems up-to-date to protect against vulnerabilities.


3. Cyber Security Best Practices:

(i)Strong Passwords: Creating complex passwords and changing them regularly.

(ii)User Education: Training individuals on recognizing and avoiding cyber threats.

(iii)Regular Backups: Ensuring data is regularly backed up to mitigate the impact of data loss.

(iv)Access Control: Limiting access to sensitive information to only those who need it.


How can we prevent from being scammed in internet :


Protecting yourself from online scams requires a combination of awareness, caution, and proactive measures. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you stay safe:

1. Be Skeptical of Unsolicited Contacts:

(i) Verify Unsolicited Messages: Whether it's an email, message, or call, if you didn't initiate the contact, be wary. Scammers often pretend to be from legitimate organizations.

(ii) Avoid Clicking on Unknown Links: Don’t click on links or download attachments from unknown or suspicious sources.

(iii) Don't click on links or download attachments from unknown sources.


2. Protect Your Personal Information:

(i) Never share personal or financial information through email, message, or over the phone unless you are sure of the recipient's identity.

(ii)Use strong, unique passwords for different accounts and enable two-factor authentication whenever possible.


3. Verify Before You Trust:

Verify the legitimacy of companies or individuals before providing any information or making a payment.

Look for official websites and contact information to ensure you are dealing with a legitimate entity.


4. Be Cautious of Too-Good-to-Be-True Offers:

Be skeptical of offers that seem too good to be true, such as huge discounts, prize winnings, or easy money schemes.

Research any offer or deal thoroughly before proceeding.


5. Keep Your Software Updated:

Ensure your computer's operating system, browser, and security software are up to date to protect against vulnerabilities.

6. Monitor Your Accounts:

(i)Regularly check your bank and credit card statements for any unauthorized transactions.

(ii)Report any suspicious activity to your bank or credit card company immediately.


7. Educate Yourself:

(i)Stay informed about common scams and how they operate.

(ii)Participate in online security training and awareness programs if available.


8. Use Secure Connections:

(i)Only enter personal information on websites with URLs starting with "https://" indicating a secure connection.

(ii)Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions.

By following these steps, you can significantly reduce the risk of falling victim to internet scams.

How to report Cybercrimes?

Reporting cybercrimes is an important step in combating online threats. Here are some ways to report cybercrimes:



(i)National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: You can report cybercrimes online through the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal. This portal allows you to file a complaint and track its status

(ii)Helpline Number: You can contact the National Helpline Number at 1930 to report cybercrimes.

(iii)Local Police: For immediate assistance, you can report the crime to your local police station. The national police helpline number is 112 .

(iii)State Women Helpline: If the crime involves women, you can contact the state women helpline number 181

It's crucial to provide accurate and detailed information when reporting a cybercrime to ensure prompt action. Stay safe and vigilant online
